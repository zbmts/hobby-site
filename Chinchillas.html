Chinchillas

Chinchilla Facts

- Chinchillas were nearly driven to extinction due to the demand of their uxuriously soft fur.
- Chinchillas are nocturnal, which means they are active at dawn or dusk and sleep during the day.
- Chinchillas are omnivores; they eat both plants and meat. Primarily, they eat grass and seeds, but they also eat insects and bird eggs when they get the chance. To eat, they hold their food in their front paws and nibble on it.
- Chinchilla fur was originally mottled yellow-gray in the wild.
- Their top speed is: 15 mph.
- Their lifespan is 10 - 18 years.
- Chinchillas are an endangered species.
- Baby chinchillas emerge fully furred and with eyes open unlike most rodents.

Reasons Why Chinchillas Are Good Pets

- Chinchillas live for a very long time. Longer then most pets like dogs and cats.
- Chinchillas are good for people who live a busy life because they only need feeding twice a day, they sleep mostly all day and they live in pairs.
- Chinchillas are affectionate.
- They have a low maintenance diet. Their food is mainly plants and grass. They require food with low fat and sugar but with high fiber content.
- Chinchillas bath themselves all you got to do is give them Chinchilla sand.
- Chinchillas barely smell if they're looked after correctly.
- Chinchillas are not aggressive unless you scare them or make them uncomfortable.
